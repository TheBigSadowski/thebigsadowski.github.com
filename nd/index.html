Why you might want me to work with you

To save you from having to read through all of my past experience on my resume, here are a few highlights that I think you will find most relevant to what you are doing at Nextdoor...

I've lead and mentored extremely high-performing lean/agile teams

At Rakuten, not only was I able to set up processes and team structure in a way that increased team output by about 4x what it was perviously, but I was able to also teach people the benefits of many lean/agile techniques, leading to teams that self-improved constantly and really embraced concepts like automated testing and continuous deployment. The coolest part about this is that some of these people have moved to other teams and introduced the practices they learned to improve the way those teams work.

I've built, run and maintained location based systems that deal specifically with neighborhoods

For example, we needed to be able to define city and neighborhood boundaries and check to make sure businesses were within the boundaries. We also needed to verify business ownership and chose to use a combination of ways similar to what you have done.

I've built two extremely high performing agile development groups

At both CityVoter and Rakuten, I've led and mentored teams in how to design a system and process to be fully confident in being able to deploy many times per day. At Rakuten, we were able to deliver new API level features to the team in Tokyo that was building the Japanese language version of the user interface and integrate their code back in every day. We used a kanban board to keep track of what stage the development of each feature was in and to be able to focus attention when things became stuck. All of this led to much faster delivery of features.

And at CityVoter, the team I built form the ground up still carries my personal best for number of successful no-outage releases to production in a single day... 26 times. I would love to beat that record at Nextdoor!
